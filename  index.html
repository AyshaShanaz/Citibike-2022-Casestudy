Phase 1: Introduction & Project Context

This case study explores how Citibike, New York City‚Äôs largest bike-sharing program, leveraged data analytics in 2022 to uncover actionable insights for improving urban mobility, user experience, and operational efficiency.
Project Goal: Analyze ridership patterns and provide recommendations to optimize service and support sustainable transportation.
Role: Data Analyst (project lead, responsible for data analysis, visualization, and reporting)

Phase 2: Problem Definition

How can Citibike optimize its service to increase ridership, improve user satisfaction, and support sustainable transportation in NYC? What are the key factors influencing usage patterns?

Phase 3: Data Collection & Cleaning

Data was sourced from Citibike‚Äôs public trip records for 2022. Cleaning steps included removing incomplete records, correcting data types, and filtering outliers (e.g., trips under 1 minute or over 24 hours). Weather data was integrated from NOAA to analyze external factors.

Phase 4: Exploratory Data Analysis

Monthly Ridership Trends: Ridership fluctuates throughout the year, with clear peaks and troughs.
Trip Duration Distribution: Most rides are under 30 minutes.
Hourly Usage Patterns: Bike usage by hour and day, highlighting commuter peaks.
Top Stations by Ridership: Stations with the highest total rides in 2022.
User Demographics: Ridership by age group and gender.
Weather Impact: Relationship between daily temperature and total rides.

Phase 5: Modeling, Results & Lessons Learned

Approach: Regression and clustering analyses were performed to identify the impact of weather, time, and demographics on ridership. Key insights include the strong influence of temperature and weekday commuting patterns.

Key Results:

  • Ridership peaks in spring and summer, and during weekday commute hours.
  • Most trips are short (under 30 minutes), suggesting use for commuting and short errands.
  • Stations in business districts and near transit hubs see the highest usage.
  • Weather is a significant driver of daily ridership.
Lessons Learned:
  • Data cleaning is critical for accurate analysis.
  • External factors like weather must be considered in urban mobility planning.
  • Visual storytelling helps communicate complex findings to stakeholders.
Recommendations:
  • Expand bike and dock availability at high-traffic stations during peak hours.
  • Promote off-peak and weekend usage with targeted campaigns.
  • Enhance weather-resilient infrastructure and offer incentives for riding in mild adverse conditions.
  • Continue monitoring user demographics to tailor services and marketing.
Impact: Implementing these recommendations can increase ridership, reduce congestion, and support NYC‚Äôs sustainability goals. Improved service reliability and user experience will drive long-term growth.
